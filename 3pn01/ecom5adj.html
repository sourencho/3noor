A well designed e-commerce site needs to be (1) Easy to use, (2) Attractive, (3) Sticky, (4) Quick and (5) Informative. Here is what each of these five terms means:

Easy to use

Can you easily find what you are looking for? Is it simple to get from one area to another?
The best e-commerce sites give you convenient ways to locate items or to look around to see if you can find products you might be interested in. You will find it easy to reach different pages within the site and to get back to a page you already visited. A good site will also make it convenient for you to discover areas you didn't know existed.

Attractive

Does the site look nice and make visits pleasant? Does it load fast and work as expected?
Images and words are used in Web sites not only to make them useful, but also to make them fun and attractive. A well designed site makes use of graphics and text to make pages lively without slowing them down or creating clutter. Icons and colors can help you know where you are within the site, or where certain links will take you, while creating a welcoming atmosphere.

Sticky

Does the site encourage you to stay longer, look at more products and come back often?
A site is "sticky" if it finds ways to keep you coming back to it, to hold your attention and to spark your curiosity. Some e-commerce sites are sticky because they have some new content every time you visit; they suggest interesting products based on what you have looked at so far; and they remember your preferences and personalize the site for your use.

Quick

How many clicks does it take to get from the home page to products you are looking for?
A Web site can be quick in many ways, such as loading quickly or being easy to understand even with a fast glance. But one of the most important ways an e-commerce site can be fast, is by keeping to a minimum the number of clicks it takes to get to a product. Well designed sites get you from the home page to an item you are curious about with only a couple of clicks.

Informative

Do you feel the site gives you enough information about the products you are interested in?
When you go to a regular store you can usually touch and feel the product you are interested in and find out quite a bit of information about it before buying. In a bookstore, for example, you can flip through a book and read parts of it. The best e-commerce sites also give you enough background information, specific data and images of the product, that you feel comfortable about buying it online.
