Iraq
The Armenian community in Iraq can be traced to the early 17th century and Shah Abbas' forced relocation of Armenians to Iran, some of these arrivals moved on to Mesopotamia. During and after World War I 25,000 survivors of the massacres in Armenian provinces in Trukey arrived in Iraq. After Iraq's revolution of 1958, political instability resulted in Armenian emigration from Iraq. The Armenia community there now numbers about 10,000.
