Great Britain
Armenian settlers first arrived in Great Britain in the 13th century, escaping from the Mongol invasion. During the 18th century, Armenian merchants went to Britain from India, and by mid-19th century Armenian traders had settled in Manchester. In the 1910s a small community had grown around London, which now exceeds 10,000 with recent arrivals from the Middle East.
