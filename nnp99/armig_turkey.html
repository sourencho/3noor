Turkey
By the end of the 19th century, most of historical Armenia was already part of the Ottoman Empire, with a correspondingly large Armenian population in Turkey. Most of the Armenians who survived the 1915 massacres were deported or fled persecution in the following decades. Social pressures caused the gradual emigration of many more. There are now 50,000 Armenians living in Turkey, the majority in Constantinople.
