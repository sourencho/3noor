Lebanon
During the last half of the 20th century, the Armenians of Lebanon formed one of the most active Armenian communities in the world. The core of that community was formed by survivors of the Armenian Genocide of the beginning of the century, and the community grew to more than 200,000 persons. During the Lebanese civil war from 1975 to 1990, thousands of Armenians left Lebanon, many migrating to the United States. Some 75,000 have remained.
