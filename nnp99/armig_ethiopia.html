Ethiopia
Small Armenian communities existed in Ethiopia as early as the 7th century, possibly due to the ties between the Armenian and Ethiopian churches. By late 19th century, Armenians had gained prominence in business, government, and diplomacy. After the Italian conquest of 1936 and the overthrow of the emperor Haile Salassie in 1974, the community shrunk to only several hundred persons.
