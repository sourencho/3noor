Russia and the Former Soviet Union
In recent centuries, many Armenians fleeing persecution in the Ottoman Empire have established communities in Russian cities. After the establishment of the Soviet Union, there was substantial emigration from the Soviet Republic of Armenia to the European and Asiatic parts of the Soviet Union, mainly for reasons of employment. Until the late 1980s, 1,300,000 Armenians lived in other Soviet Republics. The Soviet Union's demise in 1991 triggered a reverse flow of Armenians from the Islamic republics into Russia and Armenia itself.
