Cyprus
Today, the cities of Nicosia and Larnaca are home to a community of 2,500 Armenians. Armenians began to appear in Cyprus in the 4th century, but came in the thousands after World War I as survivors of the Armenian Genocide. Their community prospered until the Turkish occupation of the Northern part of Cyprus.
