Dear Teachers and Students,

Hi! My name is Anush Movsesian (they call me Ms. Anush), and I'm the computer lab teacher at St. Gregory Hovsepian School in Pasadena, California.

This is our first year involved with the Narod Network Project, and we were thinking of something fun (at least we hope you think it's fun too!) We'd like to collect Christmas artwork and Christmas messages from all the schools that are participating in the NNP.

Here's how it works: Your students create a "Christmas Card". This can be a collage, a scanned artwork (perhaps animation for those of you up for a challenge!)...whatever, as long as it's student created. Then you can have your entire class work on one message OR have each student work on an individual message. Send it to us at : st_gregory_school@yahoo.com

NNP will then post the artwork/messages on a special Christmas page! This will be great for our kids, and maybe we can even send them as virtual postcards.

Maybe this will open the door for other teacher initiated projects. Perhaps you live in a country that celebrates a certain day or event. We would love to learn about it, and what better way than incorporating students, art and friendship?

Please join in! Send your scanned artwork as an attachment to your email Christmas messages, in Armenian please :� Make sure they are in NO LATER THAN NEXT FRIDAY, December 17!

We look forward to hearing from you and making new friendships.

Anush

I want to congratulate you all on what I think was a very successful project. If you don't know which one I'm talking about ... the Christmas cards.

I would also like to encourage you to make this one of Narod's regular inter-school activities. It will be a great idea for the schools to make greeting cards or other types of cards for the different armenian holidays.

After the schools get back in from their Christmas/new year break, we should start working on DIARENTARATCH for February 2000.

The idea I had for Sourp Hagop is that I would print out Narod's "Christmas card" page and distribute it to all the teachers. With a letter we would explain to them what it is all about and invite the teachers who are interested to participate (with their classes) in a drawing or greeting card contest (I am saying contest because I think the kids would be more enthusiastic to participate. Each school would be responsible for their own contests). From all the entries received the school would choose one or two max and forward it on to the resource person taking care of this project at Narod.

What do you think?

Dear Friends,

Happy New Year and Merry Christmas. I'm so happy to say that the web site with the Christmas cards is up on the NNP2000 site. To access it, go to the NNP2000 Armenian page (http://www.narod.org/nnp00/nnphomea.html) and click on the "Soorp Dzunoont" icon.

I want to take this opportunity to thank each of you that participated in this first card project. My students enjoyed checking the mail daily and seeing the different artwork and messages that you sent.

This first project was a great way to encourage fellowship among our students and teachers that is so prevelent this holiday season. I'm not certain what the next project will be, but how about taking the challenge? We challenge all our participating schools, (especially our diaspora schools) to participate.

Oh, another thing I'd like to mention. English happens to be my first language, and therefore is easiest for me to communicate in. HOWEVER, let's keep in mind that the common language between all of us is Armenian, therefore, all messages/wishes should be written in Armenian. I know this is somewhat of a "Do as I say and not as I do" mentality, but I hope you understand. I can convey my thoughts in Armenian, but unfortunately, I didn't have the advantage of attending our fine Armenian schools, so my writing is not what it should be. (Perhaps Hourig could translate for me?)

With this much said, I will thank you all, once again, for participating and sharing with us your Christmas thoughts, hopes and wishes.

Wishing you happiness, health, love, sunshine, peace and warm friendships in the new year and always.

Sirov,
Anush Movsesian,
St. Gregory School, Pasadena, CA
