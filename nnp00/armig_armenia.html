Armenia
Throughout its history, Armenia has been the starting point as well as the destination of numerous waves of population movement. The first decades of the 20th century saw both the dispersion of Armenians due to the Genocide and massacres in Turkey, and the migration of western Armenians to present day Armenia. During the Soviet era, a policy of repatriation brought 150,000 Armenians to the Soviet Republic of Armenia from the Middle East, Europe and the United States. Since 1975, hundreds began to leave Armenia every year, migrating mostly to the United States. Armenia now has a population of around 3.5 million.
