Romania, Hungary and Poland
By the middle of the 19th century there were some 20,000 Armenians living in Romania. They had began to migrate there since the 14th century. At the end of the 19th century, many Armenians moved to Romania from the Ottoman empire. After Word War II large numbers of Armenians left Romania to settle in the Soviet Republic of Armenia. Hungary and Poland had similarly large Armenian communities in the 19th century, but these disappeared through assimilation.
