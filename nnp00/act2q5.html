In Madrid, the capital of Spain, if you listen carefully from your kitchen balcony, you can often hear you neighbour beating eggs and preparing a potato omelette for dinner. The "tortillla de patatas" is a typical Spanish omelette that usually contains fried potatos and some onions. There are no kitchens in Madrid which have not witnessed the making of a "tortillia".

There must be some very typical food or drink that you grew up with in your city. Try to describe to your fellow students the colours, tastes and looks of a meal people in your city really enjoy. Use words and maybe drawings (not photographs) but try to make it neither too difficult nor too easy to guess the identity of the city… through it's kitchen.
