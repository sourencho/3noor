Egypt
By the late 19th century, there were 10,000 Armenians in Egypt - a number that progressively increased to 40,000 in the wake of the 1915 Armenian Genocide and deportations from Turkey. In the following years, the size of the community decreased because of immigration to the Soviet Republic of Armenia, Australia, and North America. There are now some 6,000 Armenians in Egypt, mostly in Cairo and Alexandria.
