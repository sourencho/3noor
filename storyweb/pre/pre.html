Children's Books Pre-Publication Subscription

The Pre-Publication Subscription is a new approach to publishing "real" books for kids, which we are testing at the e4d institute. Would you like to participate in the effort to bring illustrated stories in Armenian to children around the world? This is how it works:

As you become involved in the Storyweb, you will find that there are many ways you can offer your help. You can send us stories and illustrations, make donations or spread the word about the Storyweb. But you can also help us publish children's books in print by sending us your pre-publication subscription for one or more of the books below. There are four simple steps to the process:

1- You fill out the pre-subscription form at the bottom of this page and send it to us online (by clicking on the "Submit" button) or just send a letter with the same information by regular mail to The e4d institute, 1220 N. Cedar St., Glendale, CA 91207, USA. Please do not send any money at this time.

2- When we receive 500 pre-publication subscriptions for any of the books, we will contact you and ask you to send your payment at the discounted pre-publication rate. A graph placed next to the description of each book shows the current number of pre-publication subscribers.

3- Once we collect the subscription payments, the e4d institute will contribute any additional funds as needed, publish the book, and ship copies to the subscribers.

4- The book will then be provided to bookstores and other distributors for sale at the regular price.

All proceeds from the sale of these book will go to cover the cost of printing. Any excess proceeds will be used exclusively to finance similar publications and Storyweb activities. Both the stories and the illustrations have been donated either by the authors or by the e4d institute.

Why do we think it is important to publish Armenian books in print?

Three main reasons:

- Some illustrated stories can only show their full value when published in print media. The details and depth of the illustrations, together with the attention paid to typography and page layout of the text, play an important role in conveying the story.

- "Real" books have qualities which we cannot replicate online. They have a physical quality and the potential to be enjoyed in groups, in the classroom or at bedtime. They can also be given as gifts, and form part of any library.

- This will encourage artists and authors who have the skills to produce Armenian children's books. Your subscription will contribute to a tradition of Armenian children's literature where there is currently a great unfulfilled need.
